# Level 4: Lead Developer

**Focus**: Leadership
**Goal**: Drive technical excellence across teams and align technology with business goals.

## Milestone

You've completed this level when you can:
- Lead a team's technical direction while growing engineers
- Balance technical debt with feature delivery
- Communicate effectively with non-technical stakeholders
- Navigate organizational dynamics and drive change
- Build high-performing, psychologically safe teams
- Handle incidents and crises with calm leadership

## Recommended Path

1. **Technical Leadership** and **Architecture Ownership** establish your technical foundation
2. **Team Processes** and **Hiring** build your team
3. **Stakeholder Communication** and **Technical Strategy** connect tech to business
4. **Incident Management** and **Documentation** ensure operational excellence
5. **Cross-Team Collaboration** extends your impact
6. **1:1s**, **Conflict Resolution**, and **Influence** develop your people skills

## Key Transitions at This Level

- From writing code to enabling others to write great code
- From solving technical problems to solving people problems
- From individual impact to team impact to organizational impact
- From being right to getting alignment
- From technical decisions to strategic decisions

## The Lead Developer Role

A Lead Developer is not a manager, but a technical leader who:
- Sets technical vision and standards
- Mentors and grows engineers
- Owns architecture and technical quality
- Bridges engineering and business
- Builds culture and processes

## What's Next?

From here, career paths diverge:
- **Staff/Principal Engineer**: Deep technical leadership, company-wide impact
- **Engineering Manager**: People management, team building
- **Architect**: Cross-cutting technical vision
- **CTO/VP Engineering**: Executive leadership

Each path builds on the foundations in this curriculum.
